Smoking in Numbers

Smoking kills 10 Filipinos every hour due to cancer, stroke, lung and heart diseases brought on by cigarette smoking. (World Health Organization)

Twenty­-eight percent or 17.3 million Filipino adults age 15 years and older are current tobacco smokers, according to the results of the 2009 Global Adult Tobacco Survey (GATS).

Filipinos mainly smoke cigarettes, which include manufactured cigarettes and hand­rolled cigarettes. Cigarettes are smoked by 47 percent of men and by 9 percent of women. On the average, male daily smokers consume 11 cigarettes per day while female daily smokers consume 7 cigarettes per day. (Philippine Statistics Authority)

23 percent of Filipino adults are daily tobacco smokers: 38 percent for males and 7 percent for females. (Philippine Statistics Authority)

According to the Global Youth Tobacco Survey (GYTS), more than one in four Filipino children aged 13­15 are smokers. Among those in this age group who smoke, 17.5 percent are girls and 28.3 percent are boys.

It is estimated that 55 percent of Filipino youth are exposed to secondhand smoke at home while 65 percent get it in public places.

The Philippines is the second largest tobacco consumer in the ASEAN next to Indonesia with 17.3 million Filipino adults smoking. It is believed that majority of them have started the vice at a young age. (New Vois Association of the Philippines or NVAP)

Every year more than 71850 Filipinos are killed by a tobacco­caused disease, while more than 505600 children and more than 15570000 adults continue to use tobacco each day. (Tobacco Atlas)

The combined revenues of the world’s 6 largest tobacco companies in 2013 was USD342 Billion, 6% larger than the Gross National Income of the Philippines. (Tobacco Atlas)

Effects of Smoking

Weakened Immune System

Cigarette smoke contains high levels of tar and other chemicals, which can make your immune system less effective at fighting off infections. This means you're more likely to get sick, and are vulnerable to even the common cold and the flu.

A Chance for Cancer

A single cigarette contains over 4,800 chemicals, 69 of which are known to cause cancer. Smoking gives cancer a chance to take over your body and increases the risks of getting more smoking- related diseases. Over 30% of cancer could be prevented by avoiding tobacco and alcohol, having a healthy diet and physical activity. Don’t give cancer a chance.

Issue of the Heart

The chemicals in tobacco smoke harm your blood cells. Smoking raises blood pressure, which increases the risk of stroke. It can also can damage the function of your heart and the structure and function of your blood vessels. Although nicotine is the main active agent in cigarette smoke, other chemicals and compounds such as tar and carbon monoxide are also harmful to your heart in many ways.

Less Life to Live

Every cigarette you smoke reduces your expected lifespan by 11 minutes. On average, smokers die 10 years earlier than non­smokers. For every person who dies because of smoking, at least 30 people live with a serious smoking­related illness. There’s more to life than the stick you light.
