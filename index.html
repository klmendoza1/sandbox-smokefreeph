Effects of Smoking

Weakened Immune System

Cigarette smoke contains high levels of tar and other chemicals, which can make your immune system less effective at fighting off infections. This means you're more likely to get sick, and are vulnerable to even the common cold and the flu.

A Chance for Cancer

A single cigarette contains over 4,800 chemicals, 69 of which are known to cause cancer. Smoking gives cancer a chance to take over your body and increases the risks of getting more smoking- related diseases. Over 30% of cancer could be prevented by avoiding tobacco and alcohol, having a healthy diet and physical activity. Donât give cancer a chance.

Issue of the Heart

The chemicals in tobacco smoke harm your blood cells. Smoking raises blood pressure, which increases the risk of stroke. It can also can damage the function of your heart and the structure and function of your blood vessels. Although nicotine is the main active agent in cigarette smoke, other chemicals and compounds such as tar and carbon monoxide are also harmful to your heart in many ways.
